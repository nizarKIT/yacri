    HYDROTHERAPY

    Hydrotherapy is a branch of naturopathic medicine that uses water in various forms (ice, steam, baths) at different temperatures to treat ailments. It has been used for centuries to alleviate pain and promote healing. Hydrotherapy works through the physiological effects on the cardiovascular, pulmonary, circulatory, and renal systems, offering therapeutic benefits such as muscle relaxation, reduced muscle spasms, and improved movement. Common treatment include compresses, packs, and steam baths targeting specific areas like the back, chest, and limbs. Hydrotherapy is particularly effective for those suffering from musculoskeletal pain, promoting healing and easing discomfort.

    MANIPULATIVE THERAPY

    Manipulation therapy involves manual treatment used by chiropractors, osteopaths, and physiotherapists to address musculoskeletal pain and disability, particularly effective for conditions like low back pain. It aims to relieve pain, improve mobility, and enhance overall function. Massage therapy, a key component, focuses on the manipulation of soft tissues using hands-on techniques to apply pressure, move muscles, and promote relaxation. These therapies improve circulation, enhance nutrition to muscles, detoxify the body, activate sweat glands, and support nerve function. Used to treat pain, stress, anxiety, and promote general well-being, manipulative therapies include chiropractic care, osteopathy, and physical therapy.
